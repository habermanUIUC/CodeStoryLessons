Final Project Outline

A significant part of this class is dedicated to having you complete a project that allows you to explore a data science technique. This document helps outline some of the guidelines and grading policies you and the staff will use.

The main idea for this lesson is to get you thinking and researching a problem to investigate, analyze and report on.

An important component of this project is for you to learn and research a machine learning algorithm (or statistical analysis). Due to time constraints, we will most likely not be able to cover the technique you might be interested in using.

The project deliverable will be a notebook and video that tells the story of your question/research, the data, the techniques and analysis, and conclusion.

Overall Grading

  • 10% Project Milestones
  • 50% Project Notebook
  • 20% Project Video
  • 20% Peer Reviews (4-5)

Project Milestones

This is simply uploading to Gradescope your project milestones. Specific instructions will come later. But it will involve uploading the same project notebook to Gradescope each time. A snapshot of the notebook will be kept at each milestone.

grading

The milestones will be auto-graded, and if necessary, reviewed by the staff.

Project Notebook Submission

You will make a final Gradescope submission of the project notebook that will contain the full story of your project. Your project is considered completed at this point.

grading

Your project will be graded by both the staff and peers in the class. The grading rubric for the notebook is given below. Your project will be peer-reviewed by 4-5 others.

Project Video Submission

You will also produce a video (e.g. screen cast, slide show) of you presenting your work. You do not have to be on-camera. Voice only is fine (with visuals).

  • You can use your phone, laptop, etc.
  • You can use any video hosting service (e.g. Google Drive works).
  • You must include the link to the video and include it in your notebook.
  • The project template has a placeholder for your video.
  • If the video is too large, it will not play in the notebook. Just provide a URL to where the video can be viewed.
  • Your presentation should NOT be you simply running your notebook.

grading

The video will also be graded by peers and staff graded (see rubric)

Peer Grading

Each of you will receive a 4-5 other projects (you can do more if you want) to grade. There will be a simple rubric for grading the notebook and the accompanying video. The grading must be done in a professional manner and submitted on time.

grading

Your reviews will be reviewed by the staff for completeness; fairness;

MS1: Project Description and Dataset

Identification of problem/challenge/question

This milestone will be completed by submitting your notebook to Gradescope.

  • write the introduction to your notebook in a markup cell in your notebook.
  • identify the issue/problem/challenge/question you are addressing
  • identify what you hope to show (e.g. your hypothesis, etc)
  • colab's markup guide
  • text cells can also include latex

Data requirements

  • at least 1000 instances (rows)
  • under 1 MB
  • load within 3 minutes
  • the dataset has to be publicly accessible (available via HTTP)
  • if you have a personal dataset, you can host it on google drive and upload it into the notebook (much like we did in INFO 490)
  • the notebook must fetch the data via
    • pandas open_csv with a URL (instead of a filename)
    • python requests library (do not use wget)

MS2: Identification of Analysis, Data Cleaning/Normalization Techniques

You will write (using another text cell in your project) what analysis you chose. This milestone will be completed by submitting your notebook to Gradescope.

  • discuss the necessary data cleaning/normalization techniques
  • discuss why you selected the analysis technique

How to grade "accurately"

Each of us will have our own definitions of what 'inadequate' or 'outstanding' is. The important thing is to be consistent AND to grade each notebook independent of YOUR notebook and independent of others that you have seen.

Don't grade relative to another person's project. It's unfair if your first notebook is so fantastic that you use it as the high bar. After each notebook you grade, reset your expectations, be fair, be consistent. Treat others' work with respect. It's okay to critique fairly. If someone doesn't put the effort in, it will show.

Notices; Remarks

Do I need permission?

You do not need approval to solve a specific problem -- use the grading rubrics and specified requirements help you decide. If you find it interesting, we will find it interesting. Let your interests drive the fun.

Good Coding Guidelines

Use good coding practices for all your code:

  • ONLY use Matplotlib's Object Oriented interface (fig and axes) for plotting. You can use seaborn (which we have not covered) but do not use Pandas to do your plotting.
  • decompose your functions. Each function should be narrowly focused.
  • decompose your classes. You can do everything using Python classes (lesson on creating Python classes will be out soon).
  • comment your code, so the reader at least knows what the function/class is responsible for.
  • use well named variables, functions, classes, CONSTANTS.
  • indent your code 4 spaces.
  • follow the pep style guide when in-doubt.
  • you can also include a markdown cell before large sections of code if you feel you need to explain it.

ASK Questions (on Piazza)

Ask all questions regarding the project, under the Project Thread on Piazza so everyone can benefit.

Incremental Feedback

When you submit your notebook on Gradescope for the milestones, the staff may not have time to review it for feedback. If you are stuck or have questions, you MUST ask on Piazza.

The staff will assume each submission you make meets the specific milestone requirements.

Integrity

Also, please don't copy any analysis that is available. Remember your work will be viewed by others and the staff. It's vital that all work you submit is from your own mind. It will serve no purpose if what you outline is just a copy&paste of someone else's blog or project.

Anonymous Feedback

All reviews will be done anonymously. When you grade someone else's work, be professional -- the staff will be able to see your comments and grading.
